LIVE Israel killed Hezbollah chief Hassan Nasrallah:

Headquarters in Lebanon was blown up with 80 tons of
bombs; Iranian Supreme Leader Khamenei was sent to
a safe place

2 minutes ago

Smoke was seen rising from a building after the attack in the capital Beirut.

Hezbollah chief Hassan Nasrallah has been killed in the Israeli attack. Hezbollah confirmed this after about 20 hours. IDF had carried out an airstrike on Hezbollah's headquarters in Lebanon's capital Beirut at around 9:30 pm on 27 September. Nasrallah was also present there at that time.

According to the Jerusalem Post, 80 tonnes of bunker buster bombs were used in the attack. After this, the Israeli army claimed Nasrallah's death at around 1.30 pm on Saturday afternoon. The IDF wrote on its social media account that now the world does not need to fear Nasrallah. He will not be able to spread terror.

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu gave permission to attack Hezbollah's headquarters from his hotel room after giving a speech at the UN on Friday. After the attack, the Israeli PM's office released a photo of Netanyahu, in which he is ordering the attack in Lebanon from a landline phone.

Tensions have increased in the Middle East after the killing of Nasrallah. Meanwhile, Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei called an emergency meeting in Iran. According to a report by news agency Reuters, Khamenei has been sent to a safe place.

This photo has been released by the office of Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu. After the speech at the UN, Netanyahu ordered an attack on Lebanon via landline. This photo is going viral on social media.

27 minutes ago

Thousands of Lebanese living on the streets of Beirut

According to BBC, thousands of Lebanese have become homeless amid
the 11-day conflict between Israel and Hezbollah. The UN agency has
built 500 shelters to provide shelter to people in Lebanon. Thousands of
homeless people in South Lebanon are sleeping on the streets, in cars
and in parks amid the bombing.
